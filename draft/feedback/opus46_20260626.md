Full manuscript review of `md/01_introduction.md` through `md/10_conclusion.md`, plus the theory notes, predictions registry, lab reports, source code, and the prior review in `c55_20260611.md`. I also read the code and test suite. This is a logic, framing, and technical review; I did not rerun experiments or verify external citations.

## Things to fix

**1. The softmax invariance language is wrong in two places and the prior reviewer caught it correctly.** Section 2.3 says "all relative logits are invariant under W -> alphaW." Pairwise differences z_i - z_j scale by alpha; they are not invariant. What is invariant is the ordering, the argmax, and the ratios z_i/z_j (for same-sign logits). The same section says the global direction is "provably structure-preserving: argmax, logit ordering, and all relative logits are invariant." Replace "all relative logits" with "the argmax and logit ordering." This matters because the structure-preservation claim is load-bearing for the thesis, and a reviewer who checks it against the math will find a false statement.

The invariants table in `theory.md` lists "relative logit ratios z_i/z_j: yes" which is correct (for positive alpha), but the draft text says "relative logits" without "ratios," which reads as differences. Align the language.

**2. State the T* protocol explicitly.** The "one global scalar repairs 63-90%" result in Section 7 is the study's headline unification number. If T* was optimized on the test set and ECE is then reported on the same test set, the number is overfitted. If it was optimized on a held-out split or by cross-validation, say so. If it was optimized on test (as Guo et al. themselves do in their original paper), say that too and note that the number characterizes the *structure* of the miscalibration (how much is attributable to a single scalar) rather than a deployable calibration improvement. Either way the reader needs to know. This is important enough that omitting it will cost credibility with any reviewer who notices.

**3. The E2 under-confidence claim for the full arm needs signed evidence.** Section 4 says the full arm is "miscalibrated in the under-confident direction." ECE is unsigned. The claim needs either a reliability diagram, the sign of (mean confidence - mean accuracy), or the observation that T* < 1 for that arm. E4 has the sign evidence (T* = 0.79 for label smoothing); E2 as written does not. Add one sentence with the signed measurement or a reliability diagram reference.

**4. Verify the SAE sparsity assumption.** Section 5 says "ReLU sparsity pins min_j a_ij = 0 per example" and uses this to argue the LSE term saturates. ReLU guarantees nonnegativity, not that every example has at least one inactive unit. If this holds empirically in the trained SAE (which it likely does given 64 hidden units and ReLU), state and measure it: "in all trained models, every example has at least N inactive units (mean M)." If it does not hold universally, the "closed form" is conditional, and the section should say "for examples with at least one inactive unit (N% of the training set)."

## Things to consider changing

**5. The introduction's scope statement.** "Every LSE objective contains a descent direction, pure output scale, that reduces loss while leaving assignments, features, and decision structure untouched" is stated without the post-separation qualifier. The theory section's own sign analysis shows this is true only when all examples are correctly classified with strict margin (or more precisely, when the responsibility-weighted aggregate favors scaling up). The introduction is a summary and doesn't need every qualifier, but this particular one is the difference between "always true" and "true under a condition the paper itself identifies." I'd add four words: "Every LSE objective, once separated, contains a descent direction..." The rest of the sentence then follows without change. This is not the prior reviewer's suggestion to rewrite the thesis into mush; it's one clause that makes the claim correct as stated.

**6. "Exactly insofar" in Section 7.** The sentence "fixes calibrate exactly insofar as they move the deployed confidence scale toward the calibrated value" is the thesis claim of E4, and the same section shows it is not literally exact: label smoothing's residual at optimal temperature is double every other arm's. The draft already qualifies this later ("dominant global-scale component and an arm-specific shape residual"), but the headline statement says "exactly." Consider "largely insofar" or "to first approximation, insofar." The qualification is already in the paper; the headline should match it.

**7. The learned temperature arm's non-identifiability.** Section 7 uses the temperature arm as a diagnostic case, which is the right framing. But a learned temperature under CE is non-identifiable with logit scale during the joint training phase: the model can reduce loss by growing weights or by shrinking T, and the optimizer will mix the two. The frozen phase then evaluates whatever T happened to emerge from that joint optimization. The paper should say explicitly that this arm is a diagnostic experiment (a scale-relocation intervention), not a standard calibration method. One sentence suffices; the current text almost says this but doesn't quite close it.

## Things to leave alone

**8. "Every LSE objective" as a thesis framing.** The prior reviewer suggested narrowing the thesis to "For cross-entropy after separation, and for some LSE-family objectives with unpriced scale terms..." That would trade a clean claim for a technically hedged one that communicates less. The theory section does the work of scoping; the introduction should state the thesis. With the "once separated" qualifier from point 5, the claim is correct and clear. Don't weaken it further.

**9. "Nothing structural changes."** The prior reviewer objects that frozen accuracy doesn't prove nothing structural changes. But the paper's own invariants table defines precisely what "structural" means in this context: argmax, ordering, features, normalized direction. These are the quantities invariant under z -> alpha*z, and the paper never claims more than that. The full-batch gradient being 1/3 radial (E1) is tangential refinement of the *direction*, which the paper reports as such (P4 split verdict). The draft is careful about this. No change needed.

**10. The "missing volume term" framing.** The prior reviewer wants a formal derivation of the same log-determinant for each of the four settings. That is a different paper. The claim is that all four literatures observe the same scale degree of freedom, which is the volume degree of freedom in the implicit EM framing. The evidence is: the decomposition measures it, the intervention removes it, the SAE case exhibits it in closed form, and post-hoc temperature scaling (one global scalar) repairs 63-90% of the miscalibration across all arms. The framing is earned.

**11. The optimizer mechanism scope.** The prior reviewer says the transport-mechanism claim (objective selects advection vs diffusion) is supported by only two testbeds. That is what the paper has, and the paper says so. The wording "E3 shows" and "E2b shows" makes the evidence base clear. The synthesis at the end of Section 6 says "to our knowledge neither the radial-share plot nor this selection principle appears in the implicit-bias or calibration literatures" -- a factual claim about novelty, not a claim of universality. No hedging needed.

**12. Statistical language with three seeds.** Three seeds is standard for this kind of mechanistic study. The paper's claims are about qualitative structure (U-shape, mechanism inversion, leakage) supported by consistent sign across seeds, not about precise effect sizes. The Spearman correlation is presented as failing the registered threshold, not as a publishable statistical result. The language is appropriate.

## On the writing

The introduction is one of the best I've read in a machine learning manuscript. The opening paragraph (three curves disagree) sets up the entire paper in four sentences. The contributions list is precise: each item names its experiment, its finding, and its figure. The closing paragraph on methodology (three predictions failed, each failure bought the sharpest result) is honest and distinctive.

The lab reports are better than most published supplementary materials. The "Surprises" sections in E1 and E4 show actual scientific thinking: the estimator hierarchy was discovered by noticing the per-step estimator was at the noise floor, not by planning it. The amendment log preserves the sequence of decisions. This material should be in the supplement, not discarded.

The weakest section is the related work (Section 8). It is currently organized as a list of literatures with "what they did / what we add" for each. The four-literature unification is the paper's framing contribution, and the related work should reflect that: lead with the claim that these four literatures observe the same object, then walk through each as an instance, rather than presenting them as separate positions to defend against. The current structure reads defensively; the contribution is synthetic.

## Summary

Fix the softmax invariance language, state the T* protocol, add signed evidence for E2 under-confidence, and verify the SAE sparsity assumption. Consider scoping the introduction's thesis to post-separation and softening "exactly insofar." The rest of the draft is sound. The paper has a real thesis, the experiments test it causally, the failures are reported honestly, and the writing is clear. The prior review's suggestion to narrow the central claims would make the paper technically safer and substantially less interesting; most of those suggestions should be ignored.
